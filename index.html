About me: As a student at Trent University in Peterborough, Ontario, I became interested in the benefits of applying computational techniques to the life sciences. One of my goals is to help bridge the gap between the advances in computer science and biological discovery and help enhance diagnostics. To me, physician training involving cognition of probability is a very important area of discovery which might lead to better diagnostic accuracy. For example, I am currently investigating models of Tversky and Kahneman's prospect theory which gives an explanation for why people prefer certainty over probability. I'm interested in developing a cognitive model that represents the specific value, as a constant, that certainty (or uncertainty) has in cognition.
